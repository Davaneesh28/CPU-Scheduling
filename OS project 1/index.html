About CalFuxk

A research project at the intersection of operating systems, machine learning, and energy efficiency

CF

What is CalFuxk?

CalFuxk is an intelligent CPU scheduling simulation system built to demonstrate how machine learning can make operating system scheduling decisions smarter and more energy-efficient. It combines classical OS scheduling algorithms with predictive ML models, dynamic hardware control (DVFS), and thermal management into a single, interactive platform.

The name is a nod to the chaotic complexity of modern processor scheduling — hundreds of tasks competing for nanoseconds of compute time, thermal constraints, voltage limits, and power budgets all colliding at once. CalFuxk tries to bring order to that chaos.

⚡

Energy First

Every scheduling decision is evaluated for its energy cost. Tasks are matched to cores and frequencies that minimize joules spent without sacrificing throughput.

🤖

ML-Powered

Linear regression runs on your burst time history to predict upcoming CPU demand. This drives proactive frequency scaling instead of reactive adjustment.

🌡

Thermal Aware

Core temperatures are modeled in real time. Tasks are migrated to cooler cores before throttling kicks in, keeping performance stable and hardware happy.

💬

Botty Assistant

Botty is a context-aware AI that understands your current scheduling results and gives specific, actionable advice about energy, thermals, and performance.

📊

Rich Visualization

Gantt charts, energy bars, and before/after comparisons make it easy to see exactly what the optimizer did and why it helped.

🎓

Built to Teach

The Learning Center covers every concept used in the system — from Round Robin basics to the physics of dynamic power scaling — with real examples.

Technology Stack
